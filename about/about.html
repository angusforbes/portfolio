I am PhD student in Media Arts and Technology (Visual and Spatial Arts emphasis) at UC Santa Barbara. Concurrently, I am pursuing a MS in Computer Science. In 2006 I received an NSF IGERT fellowship in interactive digital multimedia.

My research interests include information visualization, augmented intelligence, computer graphics, computational linguistics, and education.

As an artist, I am interested in exploring and modelling dynamic, organic multi-dimensional spaces in various mediums. I am also the drummer in various musical projects, including Chamisa Mesa, Heat Death, and more recently The Hero and The Victor and Thunderdrop.

My work experience includes programming, design, and teaching. From 1999 to 2001 I was a director of software development for Micromuse, Inc., a company that develops network management software. In 2002, I founded Synaesthetic Software to develop music education software. While at UCSB I have been employed as a programmer and interface designer for the National Geospatial Digital Archive. In the last year I have worked closely with the artist George Legrady to create a series of multimedia installations. I lecture in both the Computer Science department and in the Media Arts & Techonology program at UCSB.

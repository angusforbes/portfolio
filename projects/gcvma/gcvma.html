The Global Collaborative Visual Mapping Archive (GCVMA) is a site specific installation that invites users to upload cell phone images onto a giant projected canvas. These photos can be tagged by other users in the space. The system automatically finds connections between the photos by analyzing the phone numbers and the tags. This project was developed in collaboration with George Legrady.

GCVMA was initially presented at ISEA 2006. It was also part of an exhibition of George Legrady's work at Pari Nadimi Gallery in Toronto in Fall 2007. In Summer 2007, it was featured in Speculative Data and the Creative Imaginary, an exhibition of digital art curated by Pamela Jennings. This show was located in the National Academy of Sciences builidng and took place in conjuction with the ACM Creativity and Cognition 2007 conference in Washington, DC.
